# On-Policy Delta Distillation

**Authors:** Byeongho Heo, Jaehui Hwang, Sangdoo Yun, Dongyoon Han
**Published:** 2026-07-16 | **arXiv:** [2607.15161v1](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15161v1)

## Summary
This paper proposes On-Policy Delta Distillation (OPD²), a method for training reasoning-capable language models more efficiently by having a student model learn from the *differences* between a reasoning-tuned teacher and its base version, rather than imitating the teacher directly. The approach works within reinforcement learning post-training and is shown to improve performance on math, science, and code tasks while requiring shorter training periods than standard distillation.

## Theoretical Background
The work builds on two key ideas: (1) **on-policy distillation** — a reinforcement learning technique where a student model learns from a teacher model's outputs during active interaction, avoiding the brittleness of offline supervised learning; and (2) **knowledge distillation** — transferring learned behaviors from a larger teacher network to a smaller student by matching probability distributions. The paper identifies a limitation in conventional distillation: directly copying a teacher's output distribution may not efficiently transfer *changes* induced by specialized training (e.g., reasoning instruction tuning). The delta signal concept — the difference between two model versions — is inspired by residual learning and change-detection principles common in transfer learning.

## Core Contributions
1. **Delta signal formulation**: Instead of using teacher outputs directly as targets, the method defines the distillation reward as `teacher_output − base_model_output`, isolating the effect of reasoning-specific training.
2. **Empirical validation**: Demonstrates consistent improvements over baseline on-policy distillation across three diverse benchmarks (mathematics, science, code reasoning).
3. **Efficiency gains**: Shows that reasoning LLMs can achieve competitive performance with significantly shorter post-training periods using the delta signal.
4. **Systematic investigation**: Provides extensive ablations and evidence that the delta signal is a more direct learning target than teacher distribution matching.

## Connections to Other Work
This work relates to several areas:
- [[RLHF]] (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback) and [[on-policy distillation]] as post-training paradigms for LLMs
- [[knowledge distillation]] literature, particularly variants that preserve task-specific information
- **Reasoning-focused training** (chain-of-thought prompting, tool-use tuning) — the paper treats reasoning instruction tuning as the source of capability to transfer
- **Transfer learning** with residual representations — the delta concept echoes skip connections and difference-based learning signals
- Contrast with [[reward modeling]] approaches, which the paper avoids in favor of direct model-to-model comparison

## Practical Applications
1. **Efficient model deployment**: Smaller student models that retain reasoning capabilities without full instruction-tuning cost, enabling deployment on resource-constrained devices.
2. **Rapid domain specialization**: Training task-specific reasoning models (e.g., for medical diagnostics or legal analysis) by distilling from general reasoning teachers.
3. **Multi-stage model pipelines**: Using base + delta pairs to enable progressive capability transfer in production systems where computational budgets are tiered.
4. **Open-source model improvement**: Community-driven refinement of reasoning models by distilling from improved teacher versions without rerunning expensive RLHF.

## Product & Startup Ideas
1. **Efficient reasoning API**: A service offering smaller, distilled reasoning models that run faster and cheaper than GPT-4-level teachers, billed on latency/cost savings.
2. **Domain-specific reasoning model factory**: Automated system that takes a task description, finds or trains a domain teacher, and distills it into a production student model for enterprise customers.
3. **Model compression marketplace**: Platform where researchers publish delta signals between base and tuned model checkpoints, allowing practitioners to cheaply adapt open models.
4. **Hybrid inference orchestration**: Router service that automatically selects student vs. teacher based on query complexity, using delta distillation to ensure student quality thresholds.
5. **Education & AI tutoring**: Fine-tuned reasoning models for students at different levels, distilled from a common reasoning teacher to personalize without retraining from scratch.

## Technical Limitations
1. **Delta definition sensitivity**: The method assumes the base model is a meaningful reference point; results may degrade if the base model is misaligned with the task or too weak to define a meaningful delta.
2. **Teacher dependency**: Requires a well-trained reasoning teacher; poor teacher quality directly limits student performance.
3. **Applicability scope**: Designed for reasoning tasks; unclear if the delta signal transfers effectively to other domains (e.g., dialogue, summarization, retrieval) not tested here.
4. **Computational overhead during distillation**: Still requires running both teacher and base models at training time, which may offset some efficiency gains compared to simpler baselines.
5. **No theoretical justification**: The paper is primarily empirical; lacks formal analysis of why the delta signal is superior to direct imitation in terms of optimization landscape or convergence.

## Open Questions
1. **Why does delta work?** Can the authors provide gradient-flow or optimization theory explaining why the delta signal converges faster or to better minima than teacher imitation?
2. **Generalization across domains**: Does OPD² work for non-reasoning tasks (creative writing, factual QA, dialogue)? Is reasoning special, or is this a general principle?
3. **Base model selection**: How sensitive is the method to the choice of base model? Can you use older or different-architecture base models?
4. **Scaling laws**: How do results change as student/teacher model sizes vary? Is there a critical size ratio?
5. **Multi-teacher deltas**: Can you combine delta signals from multiple teacher models trained on different reasoning techniques?
6. **Theoretical grounding**: Is there a principled way to derive the delta formulation from first principles (e.g., information theory, optimal transport)?

## Key Takeaway
Distill student reasoning models using differences from base models, not direct teacher imitation, for faster training and better transfer.
