# New OpenAI Academy courses for the next era of work

**Source:** OpenAI
**Published:** 2026-06-12 | **Link:** [https://openai.com/index/academy-courses-applying-ai-at-work](https://openai.com/index/academy-courses-applying-ai-at-work)

## Summary
OpenAI has launched three new Academy courses designed to teach practical skills for working with AI systems, including building workflows and deploying agents. These courses address the growing need for hands-on training as AI tools become embedded in everyday professional work.

## Key Points
- Three new structured courses focusing on applied AI skills
- Emphasis on building "repeatable workflows" — standardized, reusable processes for common tasks
- Focus on agent development and deployment in real-world contexts
- Positioned as training for "the next era of work," reflecting expected shifts in how professionals use AI

## Why It Matters
As AI agents and LLM-based tools move from research to production, practitioners need structured learning paths. These courses lower the barrier to entry for building reproducible, deployable systems rather than one-off experiments. This has implications for enterprise adoption, workforce productivity, and the democratization of AI application development.

## Connections to Other Work
This relates to the broader trend of [[agents]] becoming first-class abstractions in AI systems, alongside efforts to make AI more accessible to non-researchers. It sits alongside similar educational initiatives from other labs and reflects industry demand for [[large-language-models]] training beyond model capabilities into systems design and integration.

## Key Takeaway
OpenAI is investing in educational infrastructure to help practitioners build production-ready agent-based workflows, signaling that hands-on engineering skills are now as important as model knowledge.
