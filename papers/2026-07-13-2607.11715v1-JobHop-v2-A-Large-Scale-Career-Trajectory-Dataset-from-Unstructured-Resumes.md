# JobHop v2: A Large-Scale Career Trajectory Dataset from Unstructured Resumes

**Authors:** Iman Johary, Guillaume Bied, Alexandru C. Mara, Tijl De Bie
**Published:** 2026-07-13 | **arXiv:** [2607.11715v1](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11715v1)

## Summary
JobHop v2 is a large-scale dataset of 355,000+ career trajectories extracted from real resumes using language models. The dataset fills a critical gap in workforce analytics by providing authentic, richly annotated career data at scale — something previously unavailable to independent researchers — enabling better job recommendation systems, labor market analysis, and career planning tools.

## Theoretical Background
This work builds on two converging trends: (1) the growing use of large language models (LLMs) for information extraction from unstructured text, and (2) the demand for labor market datasets in workforce planning. Prior career datasets were either small, proprietary (closed to researchers), or artificially constructed using synthetic data rather than real resumes. JobHop v2 leverages recent advances in LLM-based extraction — using reasoning-controlled inference with retry mechanisms — to reliably parse complex, multilingual resume text and map occupations to standardized codes (ESCO). The extraction pipeline is grounded in information extraction evaluation methodology, comparing model outputs against multiple annotation baselines rather than a single ground truth.

## Core Contributions
- **Scale & authenticity**: 355,315 real career trajectories from pseudonymized resumes (440K documents), multilingual and publicly releasable — orders of magnitude larger than prior open datasets
- **Improved extraction pipeline**: Redesigned LLM extraction with reasoning-controlled inference and retry mechanisms achieving 100% JSON parse rate (v1 had failures)
- **Richer annotations**: Quarter-level temporal granularity, five-level education attainment, and ESCO occupational codes (standardized across Europe)
- **Rigorous evaluation**: Measured against three complementary annotation baselines; best model trails inter-annotator agreement ceiling by only 1.1–2.7 percentage points
- **Reproducibility**: Released dataset and code publicly

## Connections to Other Work
This extends the original JobHop dataset (v1) with architectural improvements. It relates to broader efforts in [[information extraction]] from unstructured text and [[named entity recognition]] applied to professional documents. The work also connects to labor economics research that relies on occupational taxonomies and trajectory analysis, and sits at the intersection of [[generative models]] for extraction and evaluation methodologies in information extraction (e.g., comparing to multiple annotator baselines rather than single gold standards).

## Practical Applications
- **Job recommendation systems**: Train models to predict likely next roles based on historical trajectories, personalized to individual skills and sector trends
- **Workforce planning**: Help employers and government agencies forecast skill gaps, plan retraining, and identify emerging occupational pathways
- **Labor market analysis**: Economists and policymakers can analyze job mobility patterns, wage progression, and skill-to-role transitions across sectors and regions
- **Career guidance tools**: AI-powered coaching systems that show users realistic career paths based on aggregated patterns from similar workers
- **Talent acquisition**: Recruiters can better understand non-linear career progressions and identify candidates with transferable skills from adjacent roles

## Product & Startup Ideas
1. **CareerPath AI**: SaaS platform for HR departments that predicts employee attrition risk and recommends internal rotations / external opportunities based on JobHop v2 trajectory patterns
2. **SkillBridge**: B2B tool for governments/employment agencies to match job seekers to roles using trajectory similarity and identify retraining needs (target VDAB-like public employment services globally)
3. **Executive Search Intelligence**: Specialized recruiting firm using trajectory data to identify high-potential mid-career candidates with uncommon but relevant skill combinations (e.g., tech → finance → healthcare)
4. **Education-to-Jobs Pipeline**: EdTech company that uses JobHop v2 to recommend curricula and certifications based on real-world progression paths from students' backgrounds
5. **Labor Market Insights API**: Developer-friendly API selling trajectory analytics and occupational mobility data to business intelligence platforms, ERPs, and talent software vendors

## Technical Limitations
- **Language and geography bias**: Data comes from Flemish/Belgian resumes; patterns may not generalize to other regions, labor markets, or languages even though multilingual
- **Resume limitations**: Resumes often underrepresent informal work, gig roles, or non-standard careers; selection bias toward formally employed workers
- **LLM extraction ceiling**: Despite strong performance, 1.1–2.7 percentage points gap to inter-annotator agreement means systematic errors remain; no analysis of failure modes or error types
- **Temporal granularity**: Quarter-level resolution may obscure short-term roles or gaps; missing start/end dates at finer granularity
- **Occupational code coverage**: ESCO is European-centric; limited applicability outside EU labor markets
- **No ground truth validation**: Evaluation against baselines rather than authoritative labels; cannot verify extraction accuracy against actual resume intent

## Open Questions
- Where does the 1.1–2.7 percentage point error come from, and does it correlate with specific occupational categories, languages, or resume characteristics?
- How do extraction accuracy and trajectory patterns differ across demographic groups (age, gender, education level) — is there hidden bias in the LLM extractor?
- Can trajectory models trained on v2 improve generalization to underrepresented occupations or regions?
- How sensitive are downstream applications (recommendation, forecasting) to the residual extraction errors?
- Would fine-tuning smaller, open-source LLMs on JobHop v2 data yield comparable extraction quality, and at what cost/latency tradeoff?
- Can the dataset support causal inference on career mobility (e.g., impact of specific credentials or sector switches on wage/retention)?

## Key Takeaway
Large-scale, authentic career trajectory data from real resumes is now publicly available for labor market research and job recommendation systems.
