# Introducing OpenAI Presence

**Source:** OpenAI
**Published:** 2026-07-22 | **Link:** [https://openai.com/index/introducing-openai-presence](https://openai.com/index/introducing-openai-presence)

## Summary
OpenAI has released Presence, an enterprise platform for deploying voice and chat agents in customer-facing and internal business workflows. The product targets organizations seeking production-ready AI agents with built-in trust and reliability features.

## Key Points
- Presence is positioned as a "proven enterprise AI agent platform"
- Supports both voice and chat-based agent deployments
- Designed for customer-facing and internal organizational workflows
- Emphasizes trustworthiness as a core differentiator
- Presented as a commercial product for enterprise adoption

## Why It Matters
Enterprise adoption of AI agents has been constrained by reliability, safety, and integration concerns. A dedicated platform addressing these concerns signals that agentic AI is moving from research/prototype phase into production infrastructure, potentially accelerating real-world deployment across customer service, HR, operations, and other high-value workflows.

## Connections to Other Work
Relates to broader trends in [[agents|autonomous agents]] and [[large-language-models|LLM-based systems]] for production use. Competes with similar offerings from Anthropic, Google, and other labs. Builds on foundational work in [[alignment|AI alignment]] and reliability needed for enterprise deployments.

## Key Takeaway
OpenAI launches Presence, a production platform for deploying trustworthy voice and chat agents across enterprise workflows.
