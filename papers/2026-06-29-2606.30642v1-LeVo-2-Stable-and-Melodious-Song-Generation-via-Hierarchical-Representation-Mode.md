# LeVo 2: Stable and Melodious Song Generation via Hierarchical Representation Modeling and Progressive Post-Training

**Authors:** Shun Lei, Huaicheng Zhang, Dapeng Wu, Yaoxun Xu, Lishi Zuo et al.
**Published:** 2026-06-29 | **arXiv:** [2606.30642v1](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30642v1)

## Summary
LeVo 2 is a hybrid LLM-Diffusion framework that generates full-length songs with coherent lyrics, stable vocals, and melodious accompaniment by using hierarchical token modeling (mixed tokens for planning, then parallel vocal/accompaniment refinement) combined with a novel aesthetics-guided progressive post-training schedule. The work addresses a fundamental trade-off in music generation: mixed-token systems preserve coordination but lose acoustic detail, while dual-track systems improve detail but weaken global coherence—LeVo 2 solves this through staged training that separates musicality learning, controllability alignment, and acoustic refinement.

## Theoretical Background
This work builds on recent advances in conditional generative modeling, particularly the use of diffusion models and language models for structured audio synthesis. The core insight is borrowed from hierarchical decomposition methods: breaking a complex generation task into semantic-level and acoustic-level stages reduces optimization conflicts. Prior work on music generation typically uses either (1) monolithic LLM-based token prediction, which struggles with detail, or (2) raw waveform diffusion, which is computationally expensive. LeVo 2 combines language model-based semantic planning with diffusion-based acoustic reconstruction via a learned Music Codec (a neural compressor that maps waveforms to discrete token sequences).

The alignment framework draws from preference learning techniques such as DPO (Direct Preference Optimization), which is more stable than older RLHF methods. A key innovation is aesthetics-guided pre-training: rather than treating musicality as purely a post-hoc preference signal, the authors pre-train on data annotated by an automated music evaluation framework, baking in musical priors early.

## Core Contributions
1. **Hierarchical LLM-Diffusion Architecture**: The two-stage token prediction (mixed → track-specific parallel) cleanly separates global planning from local acoustic detail, avoiding the monolithic vs. dual-track trade-off.

2. **Aesthetics-Guided Pre-Training Schedule**: Automated music aesthetic evaluation assigns musicality tiers to large-scale data *before* preference alignment, providing structured inductive biases rather than treating musicality as an unstructured reward signal.

3. **Progressive Post-Training Pipeline**: Sequential application of SFT (supervised fine-tuning), large-scale offline DPO, and semi-online closed-loop DPO targets different objectives (quality, controllability, musicality) in stages, reducing optimization conflicts that arise from trying to optimize everything simultaneously.

4. **Modular Extension for Acoustic Refinement**: The Track-Specific LM can be trained independently while the aligned semantic planner remains frozen, enabling targeted acoustic improvement without destabilizing learned musicality priors.

5. **Empirical Validation**: Listening tests and objective metrics show improvement over open-source baselines and competitive performance with commercial systems across six subjective dimensions.

## Connections to Other Work
This paper extends prior work on LLM-based music generation (e.g., Jukebox, MusicLM paradigms) by addressing their structural limitations. It connects to the broader [[alignment]] literature by showing that preference learning in creative domains benefits from domain-specific pre-training signals (musicality tiers) rather than purely learned reward models. The aesthetics-guided schedule parallels work on curriculum learning and importance weighting in [[reinforcement-learning]].

The diffusion-based acoustic reconstruction is related to recent work on diffusion models for audio super-resolution and vocoding (e.g., WaveGrad, Glow-TTS style approaches). The use of Music Codec parallels tokenization strategies in other [[generative-models]] like VQ-VAE and recent work on discrete latent spaces.

The modular architecture (semantic planner + track-specific refinement) echoes hierarchical decomposition in other creative AI systems but is novel in its explicit separation of musicality learning from controllability alignment.

## Practical Applications
1. **Commercial Music Production Tools**: Integration into DAWs (Digital Audio Workstations) for lyric-to-song generation, enabling faster demo creation and A/B testing of compositions.

2. **Personalized Playlist Generation**: Real-time generation of background music for streaming services, tailored to user mood, lyrical content, and genre preferences.

3. **Game & Interactive Media Soundtrack Generation**: Dynamic, context-aware in-game music that adapts to player actions while maintaining melodic coherence and fitting mood/narrative requirements.

4. **Podcast and Audiobook Jingles**: Automated generation of opening/closing themes and interstitial music matched to spoken content.

5. **Music Education & Composition Assistance**: Tool for students to experiment with arrangements, generate accompaniments, or explore melodic variations while receiving real-time musicality feedback.

## Product & Startup Ideas
1. **MusicAI Studio**: A cloud-based DAW plugin that generates full backing tracks from lyric snippets and mood tags; subscription SaaS targeting independent artists and producers who lack production budgets.

2. **Adaptive Soundtrack Engine**: B2B service for game studios and filmmakers offering API-driven dynamic music generation with real-time scene-context conditioning (mood, pacing, genre).

3. **Vocal Coach Companion App**: An interactive app that generates custom accompaniments for practice sessions, with musicality feedback and difficulty progression, monetized through freemium model.

4. **Brand Jingle Factory**: White-label service for advertising agencies and small businesses to auto-generate on-brand music snippets matched to copy and visual mood boards.

5. **Lyric-to-Release Pipeline**: A marketplace/platform where songwriters and producers upload lyrics and get multiple musical interpretations generated, then curate and refine; revenue split model with artists.

## Technical Limitations
1. **Coherence at Extreme Lengths**: The paper does not specify maximum song length tested; very long-form generation (>10 min) may suffer from drift or repetition since even hierarchical planning eventually hits planning horizon constraints.

2. **Lyric-Music Alignment**: While the system "follows lyrics and prompts," it is unclear how tightly syllable-to-note alignment is enforced; misalignment between lyric timing and melody is a common failure mode in practice.

3. **Genre Generalization**: Training data and evaluation focus on specific genres (likely pop/mainstream); generalization to niche, classical, or non-Western musical traditions is untested.

4. **Computational Cost**: The paper does not detail inference latency or resource requirements; diffusion-based reconstruction is notoriously slow compared to autoregressive generation, limiting real-time interactive applications.

5. **Aesthetic Evaluation Circularity**: The automated musicality-tier framework used for pre-training is not fully characterized; if it embeds specific aesthetic biases, the model may overfit to those preferences rather than learning generalizable musicality.

6. **User Control Granularity**: "Controllability" is improved but specifics on what parameters users can actually condition on (tempo, key, instrumentation detail) are vague; fine-grained control may remain limited.

## Open Questions
1. How does performance degrade with song length, and what is the practical maximum coherent generation window?

2. How sensitive is the model to the quality and coverage of the automated aesthetics evaluation framework? Would a different musicality scorer produce systematically different outputs?

3. Can the hierarchical architecture be extended to multi-artist or style-transfer scenarios (e.g., "recompose this song in the style of Artist X")?

4. What is the actual inference latency, and can diffusion-based reconstruction be distilled or approximated to enable real-time interactive use?

5. How does the model handle out-of-distribution prompts (e.g., lyrics for genres not well-represented in training data)?

6. Could semi-online closed-loop DPO be further optimized by using human feedback in an active-learning loop, rather than static semi-online sampling?

## Key Takeaway
LeVo 2 solves the structural tension in full-song generation by separating semantic planning (mixed tokens) from acoustic refinement (parallel track prediction), and further stabilizes musicality through aesthetics-guided pre-training and staged preference learning—a pattern that may generalize to other hierarchical creative synthesis tasks.
