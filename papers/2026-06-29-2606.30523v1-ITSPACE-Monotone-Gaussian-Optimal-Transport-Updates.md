# ITSPACE: Monotone Gaussian Optimal Transport Updates

**Authors:** Woojoo Na, Jennifer Dy
**Published:** 2026-06-29 | **arXiv:** [2606.30523v1](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30523v1)

## Summary
This paper presents ITSPACE, an algorithm for optimizing the Bures-Wasserstein (BW) distance between covariance matrices using proximal majorization-minimization with closed-form updates. The method is designed for domain adaptation and Gaussian embedding tasks where fast, compute-efficient covariance alignment is needed, and it provably preserves positive-semidefinite structure while reaching solutions faster than gradient-based alternatives.

## Theoretical Background
The paper builds on optimal transport (OT) theory — a mathematical framework for measuring and transforming probability distributions by minimizing transport cost. The Wasserstein-2 distance is the most common OT metric in machine learning; under Gaussian assumptions, it reduces to the Bures-Wasserstein (BW) objective, which operates on the symmetric positive definite (SPD) cone — the space of covariance matrices. Proximal majorization-minimization is an optimization technique that repeatedly replaces a hard objective with a tractable upper bound (majorizer) and minimizes it, guaranteeing descent. The paper's key insight is that BW admits exact closed-form updates via square-root factorization of covariances, avoiding numerical instability and enabling efficient iteration.

## Core Contributions
1. **Exact closed-form proximal updates** for BW distance in square-root factorization space, avoiding generic gradient descent.
2. **Certificate-gap analysis** under inexact polar computations, providing explicit bounds on how deviations from exact arithmetic affect descent guarantees.
3. **PSD-preserving-by-construction** algorithm that naturally supports rank-restricted (low-rank) factors without explicit projection.
4. Empirical demonstration that ITSPACE substantially outpaces BW-gradient descent, other covariance geometries (e.g., Euclidean, Riemannian), and entropically regularized OT baselines on real-world covariance-alignment benchmarks.

## Connections to Other Work
This work relates to broader optimization research on [[Riemannian manifolds]], particularly algorithms on SPD cones (e.g., geodesic descent, natural gradient methods). It extends ideas from optimal transport in machine learning, complementing recent work on entropic regularization and sliced Wasserstein distances. The connection to domain adaptation links it to transfer learning and distribution shift problems; the unlabeled-batch setting connects to semi-supervised and self-supervised learning paradigms. The focus on lightweight inner-loop primitives parallels work on efficient fine-tuning and adaptation in large models.

## Practical Applications
- **Domain adaptation pipelines**: Aligning feature distributions from source to target domains without target labels, especially under real-time or edge-device constraints.
- **Gaussian embedding alignment**: Refining pre-trained embeddings (e.g., from VAEs or other probabilistic models) to match target distributions.
- **Federated learning**: Efficient covariance alignment across distributed clients with limited communication and compute budgets.
- **Online batch processing**: Incremental covariance updates on streaming data where only unlabeled target batches are available.
- **Few-shot learning**: Quick adaptation of covariance structure from small target batches during inference.

## Technical Limitations
- **Gaussian assumption**: The method assumes data is well-approximated by a centered Gaussian; heavy-tailed or multimodal distributions may not align well under this model.
- **Covariance-only representation**: Loses information about mean shifts and higher-order moments; combined adaptation methods are needed for full distribution matching.
- **Rank restrictions**: While supported, rank-restricted factors require a priori choice of rank; no adaptive rank-selection scheme is provided.
- **Inexact arithmetic analysis**: The certificate-gap bounds depend on polar computation accuracy, but practical guidance on numerical precision requirements is limited.
- **Scalability to very high dimensions**: Covariance matrices are O(d²) in memory and computation; applicability to ultra-high-dimensional data (e.g., raw images without dimensionality reduction) is unclear.
- **No convergence rate**: While descent is guaranteed, explicit iteration complexity or convergence rates are not provided.

## Open Questions
- **Adaptive rank selection**: Can ITSPACE be extended with automatic rank discovery or a principled trade-off between rank and BW-gap reduction?
- **Higher-order moment alignment**: How can the framework be generalized to match higher cumulants or full distributions beyond Gaussians?
- **Convergence rates**: What are the iteration and sample complexities under various problem conditioning or noise regimes?
- **Batch-size effects**: How does performance degrade with very small unlabeled target batches, and is there a principled way to aggregate across multiple micro-batches?
- **Comparison to neural-network-based methods**: How does ITSPACE compare to learned domain-adaptation networks (e.g., DANN, CORAL) in settings where labeled target data becomes available?
- **Extension to online settings**: Can ITSPACE be efficiently updated in a fully streaming setting without storing full covariance matrices?

## Key Takeaway
ITSPACE provides a theoretically grounded, computationally efficient way to align covariance matrices via closed-form Bures-Wasserstein updates, making it practical for real-time domain adaptation under tight compute budgets.
