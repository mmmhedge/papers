# How to manage AI investments in the agentic era

**Source:** OpenAI
**Published:** 2026-07-14 | **Link:** [https://openai.com/index/managing-ai-investments-in-agentic-era](https://openai.com/index/managing-ai-investments-in-agentic-era)

## Summary
OpenAI outlines a framework for enterprises to evaluate and manage AI investments in systems built around autonomous agents. The post emphasizes measuring return on investment through "useful work per dollar" rather than traditional compute metrics, and proposes strategies for improving efficiency and scaling workflows that deliver business value.

## Key Points
- Shift evaluation focus from computational cost to measurable business outcomes (useful work per dollar)
- Agents represent a new deployment paradigm requiring different ROI measurement approaches than traditional LLM applications
- Efficiency improvements and workflow scaling are critical to maximizing value from agentic systems
- Enterprises should prioritize high-value, repeatable workflows for early agent deployment

## Why It Matters
As AI systems move from task-specific tools to autonomous agents handling complex workflows, traditional cost-per-inference metrics become misleading. Organizations need clearer frameworks to justify continued AI spending and allocate budgets effectively—this directly impacts whether enterprises scale AI adoption or revert to manual processes.

## Connections to Other Work
Relates to broader discussions around [[agents]] deployment, [[multi-agent-systems]] coordination, and measuring [[optimization]] success in production AI systems. Echoes the shift from capability benchmarks to real-world utility metrics seen in enterprise AI adoption literature.

## Key Takeaway
Measure agentic AI value by useful work output, not compute cost, to drive enterprise investment decisions.
