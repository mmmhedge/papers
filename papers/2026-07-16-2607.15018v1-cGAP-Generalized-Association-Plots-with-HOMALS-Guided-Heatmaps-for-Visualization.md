# cGAP: Generalized Association Plots with HOMALS-Guided Heatmaps for Visualization of High-Dimensional Categorical Data

**Authors:** Chun-houh Chen, Shun-Chuan Chang, Chiun-How Kao, Yi-Ju Lee, Shang-Ying Shiu et al.
**Published:** 2026-07-16 | **arXiv:** [2607.15018v1](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15018v1)

## Summary
This paper introduces cGAP, a visualization framework for high-dimensional categorical data that combines dimensionality reduction (HOMALS) with heatmap-based displays. It lets researchers explore complex categorical datasets—like genetic profiles or survey responses—while keeping the original data visible and interpretable, something existing visualization tools struggle to do at scale.

## Theoretical Background
The paper builds on several established ideas. **Homogeneity Analysis (HOMALS)** is a classical technique for embedding categorical variables in Euclidean space by treating them as numeric scores that maximize associations. **Generalized Association Plots** extend this by using color to encode geometric proximity—points that are close in the embedding receive similar colors. **Seriation**—reordering rows and columns to group similar items—is a well-known technique from exploratory data analysis. The authors combine these into a single coordinated-views framework (the heatmap, subject proximity matrix, and variable proximity matrix work together) to preserve the connection between derived visual structure and raw observations.

## Core Contributions
1. **Three-dimensional HOMALS embedding mapped to RGB color space**: Unlike prior work that uses 2D projections, cGAP embeds data in 3D space and encodes it as color, reducing information loss and making visual patterns richer.
2. **Full-matrix heatmap preservation**: The original data matrix remains visible in the center, so viewers can trace visual patterns back to actual categorical values—not just aggregate summaries.
3. **Formal geometric properties**: The authors prove **barycentric traceability** (how category embeddings relate to subject embeddings), **projection-distortion bounds**, and **contrast-preservation conditions**, giving mathematical grounding to the visualization design.
4. **Integrated three-view system**: Coordinated display of the raw heatmap, subject similarities, and variable similarities in one coherent interface.

## Connections to Other Work
cGAP extends [[Generalized Association Plots]] (the original continuous-data version) to the categorical setting. It relates to classical **multidimensional scaling (MDS)** and **correspondence analysis**, which also embed categorical data in lower dimensions. The use of seriation connects to clustering and structural discovery methods. Unlike pure predictive approaches (e.g., classifiers), cGAP prioritizes **interpretability and traceability**—a growing theme in machine learning but under-explored for categorical visualization.

## Practical Applications
- **Genomics**: Visualizing gene expression patterns across categorical phenotypes (e.g., healthy vs. diseased samples with multiple categorical attributes).
- **Social science surveys**: Exploring relationships between demographic groups and multi-level survey responses across hundreds of questions.
- **Medical diagnostics**: Identifying symptom-disease associations in patient records where many variables are ordinal or binary.
- **Biological databases**: Analyzing orthologous gene clusters to find functional patterns across species and gene families.
- **Market research**: Visualizing customer segmentation data with categorical features (product preferences, demographics, usage tiers).

## Product & Startup Ideas
1. **Interactive SaaS for genomic data exploration**: A browser-based tool for biomedical researchers to upload categorical genomics data and instantly generate cGAP visualizations with drill-down and clustering.
2. **Survey analytics platform**: B2B software for market research and UX firms to visualize high-dimensional categorical survey responses in real time, automatically identifying outliers and patterns.
3. **Clinical data explorer**: EHR-integrated tool that visualizes patient cohorts using cGAP on their categorical medical attributes (diagnoses, medications, comorbidities) to support hypothesis generation and cohort discovery.
4. **Bioinformatics workbench plugin**: Standalone R/Python package + Shiny/Dash web app bundled with sequence databases (UniProt, KEGG) to visualize categorical functional annotations.
5. **Knowledge discovery for compliance**: Enterprise tool for regulatory/legal teams to visualize and audit large categorical datasets (contract terms, risk flags, regulatory tags) to spot hidden patterns and inconsistencies.

## Technical Limitations
- **HOMALS assumptions**: The method assumes optimal scaling relationships between variables; it may fail if data are truly unstructured or driven by rare categorical combinations.
- **Color encoding limits**: While RGB is richer than 2D projection, three dimensions still collapse high-dimensional variance; the method may obscure patterns that exist in higher dimensions.
- **Seriation scalability**: Reordering algorithms are combinatorially expensive; for very large matrices (10k+ rows or columns), seriation may be slow or require approximations.
- **No statistical inference**: The framework is exploratory; it does not quantify significance of observed clusters or associations or provide confidence intervals.
- **Limited guidance for parameter choice**: The paper doesn't fully explain how to choose embedding dimensionality, seriation algorithm, or when to trust visual patterns over random noise.
- **Categorical imbalance**: Rare categories may be buried in the embedding; the method may not handle extreme class imbalance well.

## Open Questions
1. How does cGAP perform on truly high-dimensional data (100s of variables)? Does color saturation and visual clutter overwhelm the interpretability gains?
2. Can the framework integrate missing data or mixed categorical/continuous variables?
3. How sensitive are results to the choice of seriation algorithm? Are some pairings with HOMALS more stable?
4. Can formal statistical tests be grafted onto cGAP (e.g., bootstrap confidence intervals on cluster stability)?
5. How do users interact with cGAP in practice? Do they trust seriation-revealed clusters, or do they need additional validation?
6. Can the 3D embedding be made interactive (rotate, filter) to improve exploration without losing the heatmap anchor?

## Key Takeaway
A visualization method that lets researchers explore high-dimensional categorical data while keeping the raw observations visible and traceable through geometry.
